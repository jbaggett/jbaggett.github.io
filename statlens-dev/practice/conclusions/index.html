Practice writing hypothesis test conclusions by filling in the blanks.

How it works

  1. Read the scenario: hypotheses and test results
  2. Select the correct contextual claim from the four options
  3. Fill in the formal conclusion blanks (reject/fail to reject, sufficient/insufficient)
  4. Fill in the practical conclusion blank (provide/do not provide)
  5. Click Check Answer to see which blanks are correct
  6. Fix any mistakes and check again, or move to the next scenario

Formal conclusion pattern

At the α = [given] significance level, we [reject / fail to reject] H₀. There is [sufficient / insufficient] evidence [claim].

Practical conclusion pattern

The data [provide / do not provide] evidence [claim].

Tips

  • Compare the p-value to α to decide reject vs. fail to reject
  • "Reject" pairs with "sufficient"; "fail to reject" pairs with "insufficient"
  • Never say we "accept" H₀ or "prove" anything
