Power & Error Visualizer

Explore how significance level (α), sample size (n), effect size (δ), and population variability (σ) interact to determine statistical power.

What am I looking at?

Two overlapping normal distributions represent the sampling distribution of the sample mean under:

  • H₀ (null): centered at μ₀ = 0 (the hypothesized value)
  • H₁ (alternative): centered at μ₁ = μ₀ + δ (the true value)

The standard error of each distribution is σ / √n. Increasing n makes both curves narrower.

Shaded regions

  • α (Type I error): probability of rejecting H₀ when it is true (red shading under H₀ in the rejection region)
  • β (Type II error): probability of failing to reject H₀ when H₁ is true (blue shading under H₁ in the non-rejection region)
  • Power = 1 − β: probability of correctly rejecting H₀ when H₁ is true (green shading under H₁ in the rejection region)

Key relationships

  • Increasing α → more power, but more Type I error
  • Increasing n → more power (narrower distributions)
  • Increasing |δ| → more power (distributions further apart)
  • Decreasing σ → more power (less noise)
